class beanmachine.ppl.examples.conjugate_models.BetaBinomialModel(alpha: torch.Tensor, beta: torch.Tensor, n: torch.Tensor)

Bases: object

This Bean Machine model is an example of conjugacy, where the prior and the likelihood are the Beta and the Binomial distributions respectively. Conjugacy means the posterior will also be in the same family as the prior, Beta. The random variable names theta and x follow the typical presentation of the conjugate prior relation in the form of p(theta|x) = p(x|theta) * p(theta)/p(x). Note: Variable names here follow those used on: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conjugate_prior
